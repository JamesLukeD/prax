What is Pre-application Advice?

Pre-application advice is a proactive approach to identifying and addressing potential environmental issues before you submit your planning application. This service helps you understand the environmental requirements specific to your development, saving you time, money, and frustration later in the planning process.

Our experienced consultants will review your project plans, assess potential environmental constraints, and provide clear guidance on the assessments and reports you'll need for a successful planning application. We'll help you navigate complex regulations and develop strategies to address potential concerns from environmental health officers and other consultees.

Why You Need Pre-application Advice

Environmental regulations are complex and constantly evolving. Without expert guidance, it's easy to miss critical requirements or commission unnecessary reports, leading to wasted resources and potential planning refusals.

Without Pre-application Advice

  • • Risk of planning refusal
  • • Unexpected additional assessments
  • • Project delays
  • • Increased costs
  • • Frustration and uncertainty

With Pre-application Advice

  • • Clear understanding of requirements
  • • Optimized assessment strategy
  • • Faster planning process
  • • Cost-effective approach
  • • Confidence and peace of mind

What are Geoenvironmental Reports?

Geoenvironmental reports provide a comprehensive assessment of the ground conditions at your development site, identifying potential contamination, geotechnical hazards, and other environmental risks. These reports are essential for understanding the suitability of your site for development and for meeting planning requirements related to land quality.

Our geoenvironmental reports combine detailed site investigation with expert analysis to provide a clear understanding of any constraints and the measures needed to address them. We follow a phased approach, starting with preliminary assessments and progressing to more detailed investigations as needed, ensuring you only pay for the level of assessment your site requires.

Why You Need Geoenvironmental Reports

Geoenvironmental reports are typically required by planning authorities for any development on land that may be affected by contamination or that has a history of potentially contaminative uses. Even for greenfield sites, some level of assessment is often needed to establish baseline conditions.

Risks of Inadequate Assessment

  • • Planning application delays or refusal
  • • Unexpected remediation costs
  • • Legal liability for contamination
  • • Health risks to site users
  • • Environmental damage
  • • Construction delays

Benefits of Our Approach

  • • Phased investigations to optimize costs
  • • Clear, jargon-free reporting
  • • Practical remediation solutions
  • • Experience with planning authorities
  • • Integrated geotechnical advice
  • • Ongoing support through development

Our Geoenvironmental Assessment Process

Phase 1: Preliminary Risk Assessment (Desk Study)

The first step in our process is a comprehensive desk-based assessment to identify potential contamination sources, pathways, and receptors at your site. This includes:

  • Review of historical maps and records
  • Analysis of geological and hydrogeological data
  • Evaluation of environmental database information
  • Site walkover survey
  • Development of initial conceptual site model
  • Preliminary risk assessment

The Phase 1 report provides a clear assessment of potential risks and recommendations for any further investigation needed. For many sites with limited risk, this may be sufficient to satisfy planning requirements.

Phase 2: Site Investigation

Where the Phase 1 assessment identifies potential risks that require further evaluation, we conduct targeted site investigations to collect and analyze soil, groundwater, and gas samples. Our approach includes:

  • Development of site-specific sampling strategy
  • Trial pits, boreholes, and monitoring well installation
  • Soil and groundwater sampling
  • Ground gas and vapor monitoring
  • Laboratory analysis of samples
  • Geotechnical testing
  • Detailed risk assessment
  • Refined conceptual site model

The Phase 2 report provides a detailed assessment of actual contamination levels, comparing them to relevant screening values and evaluating the risks to human health, water resources, and the wider environment.

Phase 3: Remediation Strategy

If the site investigation identifies unacceptable risks that require remediation, we develop a practical and cost-effective remediation strategy tailored to your development plans. This includes:

  • Evaluation of remediation options
  • Cost-benefit analysis
  • Detailed remediation methodology
  • Remediation targets and success criteria
  • Waste management considerations
  • Health and safety requirements
  • Environmental monitoring protocols
  • Regulatory liaison

Our remediation strategies focus on practical solutions that can be integrated with your construction program, minimizing delays and additional costs while ensuring all risks are appropriately managed.

Phase 4: Verification

Following the implementation of remediation measures, we provide verification services to demonstrate that the remediation objectives have been achieved and the site is suitable for its intended use. This includes:

  • Supervision of remediation works
  • Validation sampling and testing
  • Documentation of remediation activities
  • Assessment against remediation targets
  • Preparation of verification report
  • Long-term monitoring (if required)

The verification report provides the evidence needed to discharge planning conditions related to contaminated land, allowing your development to proceed with confidence that all environmental risks have been appropriately addressed.

Sample Report

Phase 1 & 2 Geoenvironmental Assessment

Project: Former Industrial Site, Birmingham

1. Executive Summary

This report presents the findings of a Phase 1 and Phase 2 Geoenvironmental Assessment for the former industrial site at Parkside Road, Birmingham. The investigation was undertaken to support a planning application for residential redevelopment.

The site has a history of industrial use, including metal works (1950-1985) and light engineering (1985-2010). The investigation identified localized soil contamination exceeding residential screening values for lead, PAHs, and TPH in the eastern portion of the site. Ground gas monitoring recorded methane at concentrations requiring CS2 protection measures.

2. Site Description and Setting

The site comprises approximately 1.2 hectares of land formerly occupied by industrial buildings, now demolished. The site is bounded by:

  • North: Commercial premises
  • East: Residential properties
  • South: Parkside Road
  • West: Vacant land

The site is generally level with areas of hardstanding and demolition rubble. Vegetation is limited to peripheral areas.

3. Ground Conditions

The site investigation comprised 10 trial pits and 4 boreholes to depths of up to 10m bgl. The general ground conditions encountered were:

  • 0.0-0.5m: Made Ground (brick, concrete, ash)
  • 0.5-2.5m: Sandy Clay (firm to stiff)
  • 2.5-10.0m: Sandstone (moderately weathered)

Groundwater was encountered at approximately 3.5m bgl in all boreholes.

4. Contamination Assessment

A total of 25 soil samples were submitted for laboratory analysis. The key findings were:

  • Lead concentrations exceeded residential screening values in 5 samples from the eastern area (max 980 mg/kg)
  • Benzo(a)pyrene exceeded screening values in 3 samples (max 8.4 mg/kg)
  • TPH (C10-C40) exceeded screening values in 4 samples (max 2,800 mg/kg)
  • No asbestos was detected in any samples
  • Groundwater samples showed no significant contamination
5. Ground Gas Assessment

Six rounds of ground gas monitoring were undertaken over a 3-month period. The results indicate:

  • Methane: Max 3.2% v/v
  • Carbon Dioxide: Max 5.8% v/v
  • Flow rates: Max 2.1 l/hr
  • Gas Screening Value: 0.067 l/hr
  • Characteristic Situation: CS2
6. Conclusions and Recommendations

Based on the findings of this assessment, the following recommendations are made:

  1. Remediation of contaminated soils in the eastern portion of the site through excavation and disposal
  2. Provision of 600mm clean cover in garden and landscaped areas
  3. Installation of CS2 ground gas protection measures in all buildings
  4. Further assessment of groundwater if basement construction is proposed
  5. Preparation of a detailed remediation strategy for approval by the Local Planning Authority

What are Environmental Impact Assessments?

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) is a systematic process used to identify, predict, and evaluate the environmental effects of proposed projects. EIAs help decision-makers consider the environmental implications of proposed actions before proceeding with development.

Our EIA services provide a comprehensive evaluation of potential environmental impacts across various aspects including air quality, noise, ecology, landscape, cultural heritage, and socio-economic factors. We work closely with your project team to develop effective mitigation measures that minimize negative impacts while enhancing positive ones.

Why You Need an Environmental Impact Assessment

EIAs are legally required for certain types of development under the Town and Country Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) Regulations. Even when not mandatory, an EIA can provide valuable insights that improve project design and sustainability.

Legal Requirements

  • • Schedule 1 developments (always require EIA)
  • • Schedule 2 developments (may require EIA)
  • • Screening opinions
  • • Scoping opinions
  • • Environmental Statements

Benefits of EIA

  • • Improved project design
  • • Reduced environmental risks
  • • Enhanced stakeholder engagement
  • • Smoother planning process
  • • Sustainable development
  • • Corporate social responsibility

Sample Report

Environmental Impact Assessment

Project: Mixed-Use Development, Bristol

1. Non-Technical Summary

This Environmental Statement (ES) has been prepared to accompany the planning application for a mixed-use development at Harbourside, Bristol. The proposed development comprises 250 residential units, 5,000m² of commercial space, and associated infrastructure.

The ES assesses the likely significant environmental effects of the development during construction and operation phases. Key environmental aspects assessed include air quality, noise, ecology, landscape and visual impact, cultural heritage, and socio-economic factors.

2. Air Quality Assessment

The air quality assessment considered potential impacts from construction dust, construction traffic, and operational traffic emissions. The assessment methodology included:

  • Review of existing air quality data
  • Dispersion modeling of traffic emissions
  • Assessment against national air quality objectives
  • Evaluation of construction dust impacts

The assessment identified moderate adverse impacts during construction and negligible impacts during operation. Mitigation measures include a Construction Environmental Management Plan with dust suppression measures and the implementation of a Travel Plan to reduce operational traffic impacts.

3. Ecology and Biodiversity

Ecological surveys identified the following key features on and adjacent to the site:

  • Harbourside habitat of moderate ecological value
  • Bat foraging activity along the waterfront
  • Nesting birds in existing buildings
  • No protected species recorded on site

The assessment identified minor adverse impacts during construction and potential minor beneficial impacts during operation through habitat enhancement. Mitigation and enhancement measures include:

  • Timing of demolition works to avoid nesting season
  • Installation of bat and bird boxes
  • Native species planting scheme
  • Creation of green roofs on commercial buildings
  • Sensitive lighting design to minimize impacts on bats
4. Landscape and Visual Impact

The landscape and visual impact assessment evaluated the effects of the development on landscape character and views from key receptors. The assessment identified:

  • Moderate adverse impacts on local townscape character during construction
  • Minor beneficial impacts on townscape character during operation
  • Major adverse visual impacts from nearby residential properties during construction
  • Moderate adverse to minor beneficial visual impacts during operation

Mitigation measures include careful design of building massing, high-quality architectural detailing, and a comprehensive landscaping scheme to integrate the development into its surroundings.

5. Cumulative Effects and Conclusions

The assessment considered cumulative effects with other planned developments in the area. The key findings are:

  • Temporary moderate adverse cumulative effects during construction phase
  • Minor adverse to minor beneficial cumulative effects during operation

With the implementation of the proposed mitigation measures, the residual environmental effects of the development are considered to be acceptable. The development will deliver significant socio-economic benefits including housing provision, employment opportunities, and public realm improvements that outweigh the identified adverse impacts.

What are Air Quality Assessments?

Air Quality Assessments evaluate the potential impacts of a development on local air quality and the exposure of future site users to existing air pollution. These assessments are crucial for developments in areas with poor air quality or for projects that may generate significant air pollution through increased traffic or industrial processes.

Our air quality assessments use advanced dispersion modeling techniques to predict pollutant concentrations and compare them with national air quality objectives. We provide clear recommendations for mitigation measures to protect human health and ensure compliance with planning requirements.

Why You Need an Air Quality Assessment

Air quality is a critical consideration in the planning process, particularly in urban areas and near major roads. Local planning authorities often require air quality assessments to ensure that new developments don't worsen existing air quality problems and that future occupants won't be exposed to unacceptable pollution levels.

When Assessments Are Required

  • • Developments in Air Quality Management Areas (AQMAs)
  • • Large residential developments (>10 units)
  • • Commercial developments with significant parking
  • • Industrial developments with emissions
  • • Developments near busy roads
  • • Sensitive uses (schools, hospitals, care homes)

Benefits of Our Approach

  • • Advanced dispersion modeling
  • • Clear, jargon-free reporting
  • • Cost-effective mitigation strategies
  • • Experience with planning authorities
  • • Integrated approach with other assessments
  • • Ongoing support through planning

Our Air Quality Assessment Process

Step 1: Screening Assessment

The first step is to determine whether a detailed assessment is required based on the scale and nature of your development. Our screening assessment includes:

  • Review of local air quality data and monitoring results
  • Identification of nearby sensitive receptors
  • Evaluation of development characteristics
  • Traffic generation estimates
  • Application of IAQM/EPUK screening criteria
  • Consultation with local authority environmental health officers

The screening assessment provides a clear recommendation on whether a detailed assessment is required. For smaller developments or those in areas with good air quality, a screening assessment may be sufficient to satisfy planning requirements.

Step 2: Detailed Assessment

If the screening assessment indicates potential significant impacts, we conduct a detailed assessment using advanced dispersion modeling. This includes:

  • Collection of baseline air quality data
  • Traffic data analysis and emission calculations
  • Dispersion modeling using ADMS-Roads or similar software
  • Prediction of pollutant concentrations (NO₂, PM₁₀, PM₂.₅)
  • Comparison with air quality objectives
  • Assessment of construction phase impacts
  • Evaluation of significance using IAQM/EPUK guidance

The detailed assessment provides a comprehensive analysis of potential air quality impacts, identifying any areas where national air quality objectives may be exceeded and quantifying the development's contribution to local pollution levels.

Step 3: Mitigation Strategy

Based on the assessment findings, we develop a tailored mitigation strategy to address any significant impacts. This may include:

  • Construction dust management measures
  • Design modifications to protect future residents
  • Ventilation systems with appropriate filtration
  • Electric vehicle charging infrastructure
  • Travel plan measures to reduce traffic impacts
  • Green infrastructure to improve local air quality
  • Emissions controls for combustion sources
  • Monitoring and management plans

Our mitigation strategies focus on practical, cost-effective solutions that can be readily implemented and maintained throughout the life of the development. We work closely with your design team to integrate these measures into the overall project design.
